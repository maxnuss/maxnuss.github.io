Max Nussenbaum

Castle was a tech-powered property management service I cofounded in 2014. We grew to $1mm ARR, got into Y Combinator (W16), and raised $4mm from Khosla Ventures, SV Angel, and Stripe cofounder John Collison, among others.

As CEO, I led product, design, and marketing. I designed (and built the front-end for) the first versions of our product, personally closed our first $200k in ARR, and grew the team from three to 18.

Through our web platform, property owners could track their portfolio’s performance; monitor maintenance issues, solicit and compare bids, and approve work requests; market their properties and monitor their tenant pipeline; and control their property’s finances. The platform also provided tools for tenants and maintenance providers.

The Castle app was used by ~400 property owners, ~2,000 tenants, ~100 maintenance providers, and tens of thousands of applicants. We measured success of the platform by engagement with the app, reduction in support tickets, and customer satisfaction scores.

Castle experienced strong growth for a few years, but we were ultimately unable to effectively scale the company’s on-the-ground operations. We sold off the brand and some core technology assets and wound down the company in 2018.
